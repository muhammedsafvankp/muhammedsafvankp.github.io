How to Access Our Periodicals

Physical Copies

Most of our latest magazines and journals are available in the Main Reading Room, located on the ground floor. Scholarly journals can be found in their respective subject sections (e.g., Science, History, Arts).

  • Current Issues: Available for in-library use only. Please do not remove from the reading room.
  • Back Issues: Many previous issues can be checked out for a period of 7 days with your library card. Please inquire at the circulation desk.
  • Finding Aids: Use the "Library Location" information on each periodical's detail page for precise shelving.

Digital Access

We offer a wide selection of digital magazines and scholarly journals through our online databases. You will need your valid library card number to access these resources from home.

  • Digital Magazines (e.g., Flipster, Zinio): Access Digital Magazines Here. Browse popular titles on your computer or mobile device.
  • Scholarly Journals (e.g., JSTOR, Project MUSE): Access Scholarly Journals Here. Full-text articles for research and academic study.
  • Need help? Visit our Contact Us page or speak to a librarian for assistance with digital access.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How often are new magazines added to the website?

We strive to update our website with new arrivals weekly, typically by Monday morning.

Can I request a new magazine subscription for the library?

Yes! We welcome suggestions. Please use our feedback form to submit your request, and our acquisitions team will review it.

What if I can't find a specific issue or periodical?

Please check the "Library Location" on the periodical's detail page. If you still can't find it, ask a librarian at the reference desk, and we'll be happy to assist you.

Are all periodicals available for digital access?

No, not all. While we aim to provide broad digital access, some titles are only available in physical format due to publisher restrictions. Check the individual periodical's detail page for specific access options.
